Marketing for Local Service Businesses in Belgium

Sebastian C. July 2026 5 min read

Notaries, clinics, wellness practices, repair shops, salons, anything where customers book an appointment and choose based on proximity, share the same underlying marketing problem: almost every search that matters is local, and almost every local business underinvests in showing up for it.

Google Business Profile is the highest-leverage channel you have

For a local, appointment-based business, a complete, accurate Google Business Profile often drives more real bookings than the website itself: correct category, consistent name and address everywhere you're listed, real reviews, and photos. It's also usually the fastest thing to fix, most local businesses we look at have an incomplete or outdated profile costing them visibility for free.

Two real examples, two different starting points

We took a Brussels notary office from invisible on Google to a 200% increase in online appointment requests, entirely through local SEO and a website structured around real search intent, no paid ads involved. Separately, a Traditional Chinese Medicine practice in Schaerbeek had no website at all, we built one from scratch plus local SEO for Schaerbeek and Brussels searches, and it generated 30 bookings directly. Different starting points, same underlying approach: be the clearest, most complete, most locally specific answer to the exact question someone is typing.

A generic services page underperforms a specific one

"We offer quality service" ranks for nothing. A page built around the actual treatments, acts, or services you perform, in the language and city your clients search in, ranks for the searches that convert. This is the single most common gap we see in local service businesses: the services exist, the website just doesn't say them the way people search for them.

Frequently asked questions

What's the fastest way to improve local search visibility?

Complete and correct your Google Business Profile: accurate category, consistent name and address across every listing, and real reviews. It's usually the highest-impact, lowest-effort fix available, and most local businesses have left it incomplete.

Do I need paid ads if I run a local service business?

Not necessarily. Our notary case study reached a 200% increase in appointment requests through local SEO alone, no paid ads. Paid ads can accelerate results, but a well-built local presence often carries most of the weight on its own.

My business has no online presence at all, where do we start?

Start with the website and Google Business Profile together, structured around what your actual clients search for locally. That's exactly the starting point we had with the Acupuncture Brussels practice: zero online presence to 30 bookings, built from nothing.
