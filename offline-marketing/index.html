Offline Marketing

Breaking through the digital noise.

Digital marketing is hitting a ceiling — ignored ads, rising costs, and inboxes nobody opens. A physical flyer in someone's hand requires 21% less cognitive effort to process than a digital ad [Mediaposte], and creates a stronger memory. It's marketing your customer can actually hold.

The Problem

Your customers are exhausted by screens.

"Digital fatigue" isn't a buzzword — it's a measurable shift in behavior. People are drowning in emails, feeds, and ads that all look the same, and they've started tuning out or actively blocking them.

4 in 10 people block digital ads

Ad blockers are now mainstream, not a niche technical habit [MBE France] — a meaningful share of your digital audience never sees your ad at all, no matter how good the targeting is.

Inboxes are a losing battle

Email open rates for cold or promotional sends routinely sit at 20-30% [DirectMail.io] — and that's before anyone actually reads past the subject line.

Attention is the real cost

Digital banner ads hold attention for a fraction of a second. The channel isn't broken — it's just crowded, and it rewards whoever can still cut through.

The Shift

Marketing you can actually hold.

Offline marketing is any promotion that reaches someone in the physical world — a flyer, a piece of direct mail, a poster — instead of on a screen. It's not a step backwards; it's the channel that isn't fighting for space in an already-full inbox.

You've felt this work before — you just didn't call it marketing.

That Uber Eats flyer that showed up in your mailbox the week you moved apartments wasn't random — it's one of the most consistent, highest-performing acquisition channels food delivery apps use, precisely because it lands at the exact moment someone is deciding where to order from. The same logic works for a local business, a service company, or an e-commerce brand — the flyer just needs to reach the right street, at the right moment, with the right offer.

"Phygital" [Taggbox] — the part that makes this measurable. Every flyer we design carries a unique QR code or promo code. When someone scans it or uses the code at checkout, it shows up in your reporting exactly like a digital ad click would — so you're not just hoping the campaign worked, you're watching it happen.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Does offline marketing still work in 2026?

Yes — arguably more than it has in years. Roughly 4 in 10 internet users now block digital ads, and inboxes are more crowded than ever, which is pushing engagement rates down across most digital channels. Physical mail doesn't compete with a spam filter or an ad blocker: open rates for direct mail run 80-90%, compared to 20-30% for email, and it requires measurably less cognitive effort to process than a digital ad.

How do you track results from flyer distribution if it's not digital?

Every flyer we design carries a unique QR code or promo code tied to your campaign. When someone scans it or uses the code, we see it in the same dashboard as your other channels — scans, conversions, and which streets or neighborhoods actually performed. It's physical distribution with digital-level measurement, not a guess.

What areas in Belgium do you cover for flyer distribution?

We run street and mailbox distribution across Brussels, Wallonia, and Flanders, targeted down to the neighborhood level based on your actual customer profile, not a generic radius around your address.
