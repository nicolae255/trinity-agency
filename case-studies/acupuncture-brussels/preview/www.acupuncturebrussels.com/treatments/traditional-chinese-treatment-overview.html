What Is Traditional Chinese Medicine?

Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) is a holistic healthcare system developed over thousands of years in China. It focuses on balancing the body’s Qi (vital energy), Blood (Xue), and Yin-Yang relationship to support health, prevent disease, and restore natural harmony.

Moreover

TCM includes multiple treatment methods such as acupuncture, moxibustion, cupping therapy, Gua Sha, meridian massage (Tui Na), and Chinese herbal medicine. Each therapy is tailored to the individual’s constitution, symptoms, and overall energy patterns.

Core Principles of TCM

TCM is built on several key principles that guide diagnosis and treatment.

Balance of Yin and Yang: Health is achieved when Yin and Yang energies are in harmony. Imbalance may lead to physical or emotional issues.

Flow of Qi: Qi is the vital life energy circulating through the body’s meridians. TCM therapies aim to keep Qi moving freely to maintain health.

Blood and Organ Function: In TCM, Blood (Xue) nourishes tissues and supports organ function. Imbalances in Blood or Qi can affect overall wellbeing.

TCM Diagnosis: Diagnosis uses several key techniques, including pulse reading, tongue observation, and patient history and symptom patterns. This individualized approach ensures treatments are tailored to your unique constitution.
